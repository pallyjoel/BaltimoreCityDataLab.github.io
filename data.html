Data Resources

A primary objective of the BCDL is to provide access to data of critical interest to the improvement of Baltimore City. This page will provide access to data curated by the BCDL as well as information about other available data resources such as the Baltimore Open Data portal.

Baltimore Neighborhood Indicators Alliance The Baltimore Neighborhood Indicators Alliance-Jacob France Institute at the University of Baltimore (known as BNIA-JFI) is a nonprofit organization whose core mission is to provide open access to meaningful, reliable, and actionable data about, and for, the City of Baltimore and its communities. BNIA-JFI builds on and coordinates the related work of citywide nonprofit organizations, city and state government agencies, neighborhoods, foundations, businesses, and universities to support and strengthen the principle and practice of well informed decision making for change toward strong neighborhoods, improved quality of life, and a thriving city. JHU Center for a Livable Future The Center for a Livable Future is to promotes research, development and communication of information about the complex interrelationships among diet, food production, environment and human health. It operates the Maryland Food System Map, which includes a comprehensive colleciton of datasets on how food is produced, distributed and consumed in the state of Maryland. D F IP IP B
MD Report Card The State of Maryland Department of Education releases an annual Maryland Report Card which colects information on school benchmarks and demographic data for all schools in the Maryland State Education System. E D IP
Open Data Portal (MD) Maryland's open data project, serving as a central location for the state's public data. The goal is to increase transparency and accountability by providing residents an unprecedented level access to government data. E T D F T PS IP H B
Open Baltimore A data collection and consolidation and distribution effort by the City of Baltimore and the Mayor's Office of Information Technology. The goal of OpenBaltimore is to provide, to the public, access to City data in an effort that supports government transparency, openness and innovative uses that will help improve the lives of Baltimore residents, visitors and businesses through use of technology.
